July 28th, 2022

I wish my mother was not ruined by those in her life. She is strong, but strong like
bamboo - breaking with too much tension on a focal point. She tells me not to complain of my
father, because he is my father - even though he is not the husband she deserves, and nor is
her own father. She deserves a life where she is not tossed around - by her family, her friends,
by me.

I try to understand her. Be her vent to let out dusty air flowing inside her. But she is smarter
than I. She is too smart because she knows I should not be emotionally burdenned by her pain,
even though I want to. She cares for me too much to place her burdens on mine as they placed their
burdens on her. She does not want me to feel an infinite heavyweight, suffocating as the pressure of the
waves becomes too much to gasp through, as she has felt her whole life.

She does not want me to drown as she is while on land.

She smiles when I laugh, even at the jokes she does not understand. She cries at night when her husband
selfishly cuts her children off from the resources they need to live, but then cries openly when he jokes
about using those same resources for his own desires. She cannot understand how someone cannot afford to
buy her groceries that she cooks her children her home-made food with, but can buy himself t-shirts, and
laud about a new car.

I know she must think she did something wrong in a past life. I want to hold her, console her - tell her
that she deserves more, that she has done too much to not. That even if she hadn't done too much, she still
would deserve more.

I am sorry to her. For living the life she could not. For having the eternal friend that she lost too early.
For being able to walk away when she must stay and silently suffer. For laughing at her with him.
All of which she endures with a smile.

I am sorry Mama - I am crying as I write this, but I know that that recent day of my birth was the one time I had the
courage to say this all to you. I don't think I'll be able to again, and I'm not even the one suffering.
I am sleeping with your jacket right now - I'll take it with me too. The smell of you has faded from it, but
I can still feel myself in your lap when I close my eyes, petting my hair and kissing my forehead when I pretend
to be sleeping.

I love you so much Mama - I hope I can give you a better life soon.

April 7th and May 12th, 2022

Its so hard being in such a conflicting relationship with someone you love who is
also someone who unknowingly brings you down so much. I hate that I ignore them, but
I know when I talk to them that I don’t know how it will turn out for myself. I know
they will be fine, but I don’t know if I will be, and it has come to the point where
taking that risk again is too much.

I was theirs. Now, I am me, stepping into being theirs when I touch them. Getting old
seems to only comes with turbulence, and I don’t know how to balance it. My morals and
my life and my expectations and my pressures and me - its becoming a lot to handle. One
act of kindness makes me tear up, and I know I don’t even deserve that much.

…

I give them love now. It has become conditional, but it is there. It has returned and it
is starting to blossom. But I won’t let it bloom. I have done that before, and it has hurt
too much to be cut up at such a prime. So, I will enjoy my growing, and hope that they do
as well. Or, I will hope to be able to ignore their stagnancy.
